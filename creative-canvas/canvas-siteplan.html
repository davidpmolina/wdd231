Site Plan: My Creative Canvas

1. Site Name

Site Name: "My Creative Canvas: An Online Art Hub"

Description: This name signifies a platform where artists to share their creations and for aspiring artists to hone their skills through tutorials and tips. It was chosen to clearly communicate the site's focus on fostering artistic expression and growth.

2. Site Purpose

Purpose: The site aims to be a platform for artists to share their creations and for aspiring artists to hone their skills through tutorials and tips.

Services and Information:

  • A vibrant and inviting introduction to the platform, emphasizing its mission to celebrate creativity and foster artistic growth.
  • A dynamic section displaying a rotating selection of stunning user-submitted artwork to immediately capture visitors' attention and inspire them.
  • Clear prompts encouraging visitors to "Explore Art," "Upload Your Own," or "Learn New Skills".
  • A concise summary of what the site offers (showcasing, learning, community).
  • Tutorials organized by medium (e.g., Digital Painting, Traditional Drawing, Sculpture, Watercolor), skill level (Beginner, Intermediate, Advanced), or subject matter (Portraits, Landscapes, Still Life).
  • A dedicated area for short, actionable advice on various artistic techniques, tools, or creative blocks.
  • Links to helpful external resources like free brushes, reference photo sites, or recommended art supplies.
  • Simple, clear instructions for artists to submit their work.
  • Basic rules for respectful interaction and constructive feedback within the platform.
  • An email address for inquiries, feedback, or tutorial requests.

3. Scenarios

Scenarios:

  • Where can I find stunning user-submitted artwork for inspiration?
  • How can I categorize featured art, such as by "Art of the Week" or "New Arrivals"?
  • What concise summary describes the site's offerings in terms of showcasing, learning, and community?
  • Where can I find tutorials organized by skill level (Beginner, Intermediate, Advanced) or subject matter (Portraits, Landscapes, Still Life)?
  • How can artists submit their work, including guidelines on image format, resolution, and the submission process (e.g., "Email us your high-resolution images with a short artist statement")?

4. Color Schema

Primary Color: #000000 (Black) - Used for headings and calls to action. It represents creativity and passion.

Secondary Color: #D2B48C (Tan) - Used for backgrounds and complementary elements, symbolizing the organic nature of art.

Accent Color: #FFE66D (Soft Yellow) - Used for highlights and subtle accents, evoking inspiration and warmth.

5. Typography

Font 1: 'Montserrat', sans-serif - Used for headings and titles to convey a modern and artistic feel.

Font 2: 'Open Sans', sans-serif - Used for body text and general content, ensuring readability and a clean aesthetic.
